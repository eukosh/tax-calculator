Austrian Tax Treatment of US Nicht-Meldefonds (Realty Income REIT & SCHD ETF) — Personal Reference
Both Realty Income and SCHD are classified as Nicht-Meldefonds under Austrian law (§40 Abs 2 Z 2 ImmoInvFG for REITs, §186 Abs 2 Z 3 InvFG for equity ETFs) and must be declared via E1kv as part of the Einkommensteuererklärung.
Year-end lump sum (pauschale AgE) — Kennzahl 937: Each year at 31.12., a deemed income is calculated as max(90% of price gain Jan1→Dec31, 10% of Dec31 value) × 27.5% KESt. This amount goes into Kennzahl 937 and increases the tax cost basis of the position to prevent double taxation on eventual sale.
Actual distributions — Kennzahl 898: All distributions received during the year are fully taxable at 27.5% on the gross amount and go into Kennzahl 898. They do not adjust the cost basis. Legal basis: §40 Abs 2 Z 2 ImmoInvFG (REITs), §186 Abs 2 Z 3 InvFG (ETFs).
US withholding tax credit — Kennzahl 998: The 15% US withholding tax already deducted at source on both REIT distributions and ETF dividends can be credited against Austrian tax liability via Kennzahl 998. For Realty Income the legal basis is InvFR 2018 RZ 594 (Art. 10 OECD-MA, Streubesitz/intransparent treatment at treaty level). For SCHD the legal basis is InvFR 2018 RZ 543 and RZ 546 (DBA-konforme Anrechnung im Rahmen der Veranlagung when the Auslands-KESt VO 2012 mechanism was not applied by the broker). The credit is capped at 15% per the US-Austria DBA. IBKR Activity Statement showing gross distribution and WHT deducted should serve as Nachweis; confirm acceptance with your local Finanzamt in writing via FinanzOnline before filing.
Net effect on distributions: Gross distribution × 27.5% − 15% WHT already paid = 12.5% still owed to Austria, giving an effective total burden of 27.5% rather than 42.5%.
